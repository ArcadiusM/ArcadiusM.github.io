Scale-city simulation and generation scheme of traffic organisation

Bachelor thesis at Warsaw University of Technology written in Polish. Thesis included two parts: prediction of traffic intensity and simulating traffic in the city. I defended my thesis and graduated from WUT with 4.5 grade (Polish grading system max. 5)

Project: Scale-city simulation and generation scheme of traffic organisation
What I have learned or improved in my knowledge:

RStudio, R, Matlab, Shiny, ARIMA, Time series, Nagel-Schreckenberg mode

Abstract

In the present world, when car vehicles are a publicly available good, it can be stated that almost every person has met with traffic jams as a pedestrian or a driver. The expanded urban agglomerations do not allow for the expansion of the road network, but modern traffic management systems come to the city to better organize traffic flows. In the work titled "Simulations and generations of traffic organization schemes in the city scale" algorithms and models that could be useful in two modules of such a system were presented. The first module would be responsible for the prediction of traffic intensity, and the second for simulating traffic in the city. For the prediction of data, a mathematical and statistical autoregressive integrated moving average, otherwise called the ARIMA model, was proposed, while cellular automaton were used to model the traffic flows. Three cellular automata were presented as an extension of the Nagel-Schreckenberg model, which is used in traffic engineering to simulate motorway traffic. For research in the diploma thesis most algorithms have been implemented using the R programming language and the MATLAB program. While testing the correctness of forecasting the ARIMA model, time series were used which constitute historical data of traffic intensity at the intersections of the road No. 91 with streets in three cities in the Pomeranian Voivodship. These data were taken from publicly available information published by the General Directorate of National Roads and Motorways. In this work you can not only familiarize yourself with the proposed algorithms, but also with the basic concepts of traffic engineering, the basics of time series analysis and cellular automata.

In this work it was undertaken to propose algorithms that could be used in two modules of the urban traffic management system. The first one would be responsible for generating both current and future traffic volumes, while the second would be used to simulate vehicle traffic. These two modules would form an integral part of the overall system.

Project includes three parts:

  • General introduction - introduces the general subject of traffic engineering. The basic concepts in road traffic are discussed, such as traffic volume, capacity and speed. Next, the general subject of traffic modelling and briefly the Integrated Traffic Management System in Warsaw is presented.
  • Traffic volume predictions - second part is fully devoted to traffic prediction. The method proposed for data prediction is a mathematical-statistical model for the analysis of historical data in a time series. The first part of this chapter introduces the subject of time series and the proposed model for series representation and forecasting, and the second part shows the authentic research carried out for the work for three intersections in the Pomeranian Voivodeship.
  • Cellular automata part - third part presents the introduction to cellular automats and their subsequent use in traffic modelling. One of the most important automats in traffic simulation is given and an extended model is proposed, which already reflects the basic features of traffic in an urban agglomeration.
